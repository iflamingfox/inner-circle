artistBio: |
  Born in 1986, **Rimpa Siva** is among India's most celebrated tabla artists — and
  one of remarkably few women to have made the solo tabla her own. She was the
  subject of the 1999 French documentary *Rimpa Siva: Princess of Tabla*, the film
  that gave her the name she is still known by.

  A prodigy, she gave her first stage performance in Kolkata at the age of eight and
  was touring the United States while still a schoolgirl, with later concerts across
  the Netherlands and the United Kingdom. She holds a master's degree from Rabindra
  Bharati University, and has been honoured with the Sangeet Natak Akademi Award and
  the Indian Music Academy's President Award.

  Her playing is marked by a rare combination of technical precision, spontaneity,
  and sheer expressive power — the tabla, in her hands, is not an accompanist's
  instrument but the voice that carries the evening.

essayBody: |
  The tabla is a pair of hand drums — the wooden *dayan* under the right hand and the
  metal *bayan* under the left — capable of an extraordinary vocabulary of timbre and
  pitch. A solo recital is its own architecture: it opens with a slow, expansive
  *peshkar*, then builds through composed and improvised forms — *gats*, *tukras*,
  and *relas* — each a study in rhythm, surprise, and resolution.

  The Farukhabad gharana, Rimpa Siva's school, is known for the beauty and breadth of
  its compositions and for a flowing, melodic touch. In a room this small, every
  stroke is audible — the whisper of the fingertips as clearly as the thunder of the
  full hand.

We are thrilled to welcome **Rimpa Siva** for an evening of solo tabla. Her
performances open with a long, meditative *peshkar* that gradually gives way to an
electrifying solo — moving through *gats*, *tukras*, and *relas* with a clarity that
makes the most intricate rhythm feel inevitable.

She is accompanied by **Shreyes Ravi** on the harmonium, whose held melodic line
gives the tabla its sky to move against. Come early, settle in, and let the cycles
of rhythm carry the room.
