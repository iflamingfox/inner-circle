  Born in Kolkata, **Rhitom Sarkar** began his musical journey at the age of nine.

essayBody: |
  The Indian classical slide guitar is a guitar reimagined: laid flat across the lap,
  its action raised, and played not on the frets but with a polished steel bar that
  the hand glides along the strings. That glide is everything — it lets the guitar do
  what fretted instruments cannot: produce *meend*, the continuous, vocal slide
  between notes that lies at the very heart of raga.

  Brought into Hindustani music by pioneers such as Brij Bhushan Kabra and Rhitom's
  own guru, Pt. Debashish Bhattacharya, the instrument sits between a sitar's
  resonance and a steel guitar's sustain. Rhitom adds one more thread — the
  fingerstyle articulation of the Spanish guitar — woven into the Hindustani
  *gayaki ang*, the "singing style."

Join us for an intimate, living-room concert featuring one of Hindustani classical
music's most distinctive voices. This is a "pin-drop" kind of evening — a rare chance
to experience the depth and nuance of Indian classical music from just a few feet
away, in the warmth of a private home.

Rhitom is joined by **Debu Nayak on tabla**, whose intricate finger-work creates a
living dialogue with the sliding tones of the guitar. There is no stage and there are
no barriers — only the natural resonance of the instruments in a quiet room.
