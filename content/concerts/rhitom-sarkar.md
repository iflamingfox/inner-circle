Join us for an intimate, living-room concert featuring one of Hindustani classical music's most distinctive voices. This is a "pin-drop" kind of evening — a rare chance to experience the depth and nuance of Indian classical music from just a few feet away, in the warmth of a private home.

### The Artists

**Pt. Rhitom Sarkar** leads the evening on the Mohan Veena — a specialized slide guitar that sits in the sonic space between a sitar's resonance and a steel guitar's soulful sustain. A celebrated disciple of Pt. Debashish Bhattacharya, Rhitom's playing is the result of years of research blending Spanish guitar techniques with the liquid, vocal-like *Gayaki Ang* of Hindustani ragas.

He is joined by **Debu Nayak on tabla** — a master of rhythm whose intricate finger-work creates a dynamic, living dialogue with the sliding tones of the Veena.

### Why This Is Special

Experience *meend* — the gliding, microtonal shifts that mimic the human voice. There is no stage and there are no barriers; just the natural resonance of the instruments in a quiet room. Capacity is strictly limited to preserve an intimate, personal connection between the performers and the room.
