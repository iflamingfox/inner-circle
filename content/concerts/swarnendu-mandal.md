essayTitle: "On the Sarod"

essayBody: |
  The sarod descends from the Afghan *rubab*: around 1855, Niamatullah Khan of
  Lucknow stripped away its frets, faced it with a polished steel plate, and
  replaced gut with metal strings. What emerged was an instrument of glides.

  With no frets to stop the notes, the player presses the strings against bare
  metal with a fingernail and slides — producing *meend*, the continuous, vocal
  gliding that lies at the heart of Indian music. Its twenty-odd strings (melody,
  drone, *chikari*, and a bank of sympathetic strings) and skin-faced wooden body
  give the sarod its unmistakable sound: deep, weighty, and introspective, ringing
  with resonance long after the note is struck.

Join us for an intimate, living-room concert featuring one of Hindustani classical
music's most exciting young voices. Bring your full attention — the sarod rewards
it. A rare chance to sit just feet from a master-in-the-making and hear every
